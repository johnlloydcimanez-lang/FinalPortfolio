About Temperature Scales

Celsius (°C): The Celsius scale is a metric temperature scale. Water freezes at 0°C and boils at 100°C (at 1 atmosphere of pressure).

Fahrenheit (°F): The Fahrenheit scale is used primarily in the United States. Water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F (at 1 atmosphere of pressure).

Kelvin (K): The Kelvin scale is an absolute temperature scale. It starts at absolute zero, the lowest possible temperature, at 0 K. One Kelvin unit is equivalent to one degree Celsius.
